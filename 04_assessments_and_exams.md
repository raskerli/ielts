# IELTS Band 8.0 — Assessment Framework & Weekly Exams
### How to test yourself, analyze mistakes, and measure real progress

---

## THE ASSESSMENT PHILOSOPHY

Most students practice too much and assess too little. This is a mistake.

A test you analyze carefully is worth more than five tests you rush through. Every wrong answer contains information about a specific gap in your knowledge. Find the gap. Fix it. Test again.

---

## THE THREE TYPES OF ASSESSMENT IN THIS PLAN

**1. Skill Drills** — Short targeted exercises (20–40 min). Done 3–4 times per week.
**2. Section Tests** — One complete section (Listening OR Reading OR Writing). Done 2 times per week.
**3. Full Mock Tests** — Complete IELTS simulation. Done at Weeks 1, 4, 8, 10, 12.

## HOW TO RUN A FULL MOCK TEST

### Setup (Do This Exactly)
- Find a quiet room. No phone. No interruptions.
- Use only paper and pencil during the test.
- Have a timer you can see.
- Do NOT check answers during the test.

### Timing
| Section | Time |
|---|---|
| Listening | 30 minutes (+ 10 min answer transfer) |
| Reading | 60 minutes |
| Break | 10 minutes only |
| Writing Task 1 | 20 minutes |
| Writing Task 2 | 40 minutes |
| Speaking | 11–14 minutes (record yourself) |

### After the Test — The Review Protocol (This Is Where the Learning Happens)

**Step 1 — Score first**
Mark Listening and Reading immediately. Calculate your raw score and convert to band using the table below.

**Listening Band Score Reference:**
| Raw Score | Band |
|---|---|
| 39–40 | 9.0 |
| 37–38 | 8.5 |
| 35–36 | 8.0 |
| 32–34 | 7.5 |
| 30–31 | 7.0 |
| 26–29 | 6.5 |
| 23–25 | 6.0 |

**Reading Band Score Reference:**
| Raw Score | Band |
|---|---|
| 39–40 | 9.0 |
| 37–38 | 8.5 |
| 35–36 | 8.0 |
| 33–34 | 7.5 |
| 30–32 | 7.0 |
| 27–29 | 6.5 |
| 23–26 | 6.0 |

**Step 2 — Error categorization**
For every wrong Listening answer, label it:
- **D** = Distraction (you heard the right answer but a "trap" changed your mind)
- **M** = Missed (you were still writing when the next answer came)
- **S** = Spelling error (you heard correctly but spelled wrong)
- **V** = Vocabulary (you did not know a word)

For every wrong Reading answer, label it:
- **P** = Paraphrase trap (the answer in the text looked different from the question)
- **T** = Time pressure (you ran out of time on this passage)
- **NGG** = Not Given/False confusion (you inferred instead of sticking to the text)
- **V** = Vocabulary (you did not know a key word)

**Step 3 — Writing self-assessment**
Use the official IELTS band descriptors (available at ielts.org) to score your own Task 1 and Task 2. Be honest.

For each piece of writing, answer:
- Did I fully answer the question? (Task Achievement)
- Does my writing flow logically? (Coherence & Cohesion)
- Did I use a variety of vocabulary? (Lexical Resource)
- Did I use a variety of sentence structures? Are there grammar errors? (Grammar Range & Accuracy)

**Step 4 — Speaking self-assessment**
Listen to your recording and rate yourself:
- Fluency (did you hesitate a lot? use many "ums"?)
- Vocabulary (did you repeat the same basic words?)
- Grammar (any consistent errors?)
- Pronunciation (were you easy to understand?)

**Step 5 — Log everything**
Enter your scores and error categories into the Study Tracker App. You will see patterns over time.

### QUIZ TYPE 2: Grammar Quiz (Bi-Weekly)

**Quiz A — Sentence Correction**
Find and fix the error in each sentence:

1. The number of students who attends university have increased dramatically.
2. Despite of the high cost, many people prefer to live in city centres.
3. If the government will invest more in education, the economy would improve.
4. The research, which it was conducted in 2019, showed significant results.
5. Neither the students nor the teacher were satisfied by the results.
6. By the time the results published, the policy had already changed.
7. The data suggests that peoples' attitudes have shifted considerably.
8. Having study for hours, the exam still felt difficult.
9. It is widely believed that the environment is effect by human activities.
10. The government should to take responsibility for public health.

**Answers:**
1. "attends" → "attend" (relative clause uses plural "students")
2. "Despite of" → "Despite" (no "of" with despite)
3. "will invest" → "invested" (second conditional)
4. Remove "it" ("which was conducted")
5. Correct as written. (neither/nor takes the verb from the nearest noun — "teacher" = singular "was")
6. "published" → "were published" (passive needed)
7. "peoples'" → "people's" (people is already plural)
8. "Having study" → "Having studied"
9. "effect" → "affected"
10. Remove "to" after "should"

**Quiz B — Linking Words**
Complete each sentence with the most appropriate linking word:

(although / therefore / however / furthermore / in contrast / consequently / despite / as a result / nevertheless / whereas)

1. The government increased investment in education. __________, test scores did not improve immediately.
2. __________ the high cost of university, many students choose to attend.
3. Cities tend to be expensive. __________, rural areas generally offer lower living costs.
4. Air pollution has increased dramatically. __________, many cities are now implementing strict vehicle controls.
5. __________ some people believe social media is harmful, others see it as a vital communication tool.

**Answers:** 1. However / Nevertheless 2. Despite 3. In contrast 4. Consequently / As a result 5. Although

### QUIZ TYPE 3: Listening Comprehension Mini-Drill (Weekly)

**Instructions:** Go to BBC 6 Minute English (https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/6-minute-english). Choose any episode. Listen ONCE without transcript. Answer these questions:

1. What is the main topic of this episode?
2. Name 3 specific facts or statistics mentioned.
3. What was the speakers' overall conclusion or view?
4. Write 5 new vocabulary words you heard.
5. Listen again with the transcript. How many details did you miss first time?

---

### QUIZ TYPE 4: Reading Comprehension Mini-Test (Weekly)

**The Guardian Speed Test:**

1. Go to https://www.theguardian.com
2. Choose an article on Science, Environment, or Technology
3. Set a timer for 8 minutes
4. Read the article and answer:
   - What is the main claim of the article?
   - What evidence or examples are given?
   - What is the author's conclusion?
   - Find 3 examples of sophisticated vocabulary or collocations
5. Check your answers by re-reading. How accurate were you?

### QUIZ TYPE 5: Speaking Quick-Fire Test (Weekly)

Record yourself answering these questions WITHOUT preparation time. Listen back.

**Part 1 Questions (2 minutes total):**
- Do you prefer studying alone or with others?
- What do you like most about where you live?
- Have you ever learned a new skill recently?

**Part 2 (2 minutes — prepare for 1 minute first):**
*"Describe a time when you had to make an important decision. You should say:
— What the decision was
— What options you had
— How you made the decision
— Whether you are happy with the outcome"*

**Part 3 (1 minute each):**
- Do you think people are generally good at making decisions?
- How has technology changed the way people make decisions?
- Should governments make decisions for people about their health choices?

**Self-Assessment:** After listening back, score yourself 1–9 on fluency, vocabulary, grammar, and pronunciation.

### QUIZ TYPE 6: Writing Task Prompt Bank (Weekly Essay Practice)

**Week 1:** *"The most important thing a country can invest in is its education system. To what extent do you agree?"*

**Week 2:** *"Some people believe that technology is making people less social. Others think it is bringing people together. Discuss both views and give your own opinion."*

**Week 3:** *"In many countries, the government is spending more money on treating diseases than on preventing them. Is this a positive or negative development?"*

**Week 4:** *"Some people think that it is better to have more choices in life, while others think that having fewer choices is better. Discuss both views and give your opinion."*

**Week 5:** *"The increasing amount of plastic waste is threatening the natural environment. What are the causes of this problem, and what measures could be taken to address it?"*

**Week 6:** *"Many people argue that the arts (music, painting, literature) are as important as science and technology in today's world. To what extent do you agree?"*

**Week 7:** *"Some believe that urban growth is causing environmental problems and should be controlled. To what extent do you agree?"*

**Week 8:** *"The media has too much influence on people's opinions. To what extent do you agree?"*

**Week 9–12:** Use official Cambridge IELTS books (12–18) for Task 2 essay prompts. These are the closest to real exam questions.

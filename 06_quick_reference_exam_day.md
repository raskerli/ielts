# IELTS Band 8.0 — Quick Reference Card & Exam Day Guide
### Print this. Keep it on your desk. Read it the morning of your exam.

---

## SECTION TIMINGS — KNOW THESE BY HEART

| Section | Time | Questions | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Listening | 30 min + 10 min transfer | 40 | Predict → Listen → Transfer carefully |
| Reading | 60 minutes | 40 | ~18–20 min per passage |
| Writing | 60 minutes | 2 tasks | Task 1: 20 min | Task 2: 40 min |
| Speaking | 11–14 minutes | 3 parts | Natural, developed answers |

---

## LISTENING: 5-POINT CHECKLIST

1. **Before the recording:** Read ALL questions in the section. Predict the type of answer (date? name? number?).
2. **During the recording:** Write your answer as you hear it. Keep up.
3. **If you miss one:** Move on immediately. Don't dwell — you'll miss the next one.
4. **Spelling:** Check it in transfer time. IELTS penalizes spelling errors in Listening.
5. **Distractors:** The answer often changes. The first thing mentioned is usually the WRONG answer.

### Common Listening Traps
- Speaker says $20 then corrects to $15 → answer is $15
- Speaker spells a name: write each letter as it's said
- Numbers: forty (40) vs fourteen (14) — listen carefully

---

## READING: 5-POINT CHECKLIST

1. **Skim first:** Read the title and first sentence of each paragraph. This gives you the map.
2. **Read questions first:** Know what you are looking for before diving into the text.
3. **Time management:** If one question takes more than 3 minutes, skip it and return.
4. **True/False/Not Given:** The most failed question type. Key rule: if the text does NOT mention it, it is NOT GIVEN. Do not guess or infer.
5. **Matching Headings:** Read the first and last sentence of each paragraph, not the whole thing.

### True / False / Not Given — The Rule
- **TRUE:** The text directly confirms the statement.
- **FALSE:** The text directly contradicts the statement.
- **NOT GIVEN:** The text does not mention it at all. No inference allowed.

---

## WRITING: 5-POINT CHECKLIST

**Task 1:**
1. Paraphrase the title in your introduction (DO NOT copy it)
2. Write an overview SECOND (main trends — no numbers)
3. Only COMPARE and DESCRIBE — do not explain why
4. Minimum 150 words
5. Finish in 20 minutes

**Task 2:**
1. Plan for 5 minutes before writing
2. Each body paragraph: ONE main idea + explanation + example
3. Stay on topic — every sentence must link to the question
4. Check your conclusion restates your position
5. Minimum 250 words. Aim for 270–290.

---

## SPEAKING: 5-POINT CHECKLIST

1. **Take 1–2 seconds to think** before answering. This is normal. The examiner expects it.
2. **Never give one-sentence answers** in Part 1. Always add a reason or example.
3. **Part 2:** Use your 1-minute prep time. Write 3–4 key words, not full sentences.
4. **Part 3:** Give structured answers. Opinion → Reason → Example → Bigger picture.
5. **If you don't understand:** "Could you rephrase that?" or "I'm not entirely sure I understand — do you mean...?" This is fine.

## BAND 8 VOCABULARY — ESSENTIAL SUBSTITUTIONS

| Basic Word | Band 8 Alternative |
|---|---|
| good | beneficial, advantageous, constructive, favorable |
| bad | detrimental, harmful, counterproductive, adverse |
| important | crucial, essential, fundamental, significant, paramount |
| show | demonstrate, indicate, illustrate, reveal, suggest |
| increase | rise, surge, climb, escalate, grow, expand |
| decrease | decline, fall, drop, plummet, diminish, reduce |
| think | believe, argue, contend, maintain, suggest |
| problem | challenge, issue, concern, drawback, obstacle |

| people | individuals, citizens, populations, communities |

---

## ESSAY LINKING WORDS — ORGANIZED BY FUNCTION

**Adding information:** Furthermore / Moreover / In addition / What is more / Not only... but also

**Contrasting:** However / Nevertheless / Nonetheless / On the other hand / In contrast / Conversely / That said / Despite this

**Cause/Effect:** Therefore / Consequently / As a result / Hence / This leads to / This suggests that

**Giving examples:** For instance / For example / Such as / To illustrate this / A case in point is

**Conceding a point:** Admittedly / Although / Even though / While it is true that / Despite the fact that

**Concluding:** In conclusion / To conclude / Overall / On balance / Taking everything into consideration

## SPEAKING PART 2 — PLANNING TEMPLATE

When the examiner gives you the cue card, write these in 1 minute:

```
Who/What: ________________
When: ________________
Where: ________________
Why memorable/important: ________________
How it made me feel: ________________
One specific detail: ________________
```

Start with: "I'd like to talk about..." or "The [person/thing/event] I'm going to describe is..."
End with: "...and that's why I find [it/him/her] so memorable/significant."

## EXAM DAY — HOUR BY HOUR

**Evening before:**
- Prepare your ID (passport)
- Set TWO alarms
- Sleep by 10:30 PM
- Lay out your clothes
- NO heavy studying

**Morning of exam:**
- Light breakfast (your brain needs glucose)
- Read through this Quick Reference card once
- Arrive 30 minutes early

**In the exam:**
- Listening: Stay calm if you miss an answer. Move forward.
- Reading: Watch your time at 20 and 40 minutes.
- Writing: Plan before you write. Proofread at the end.
- Speaking: Smile. Breathe. Speak to the examiner, not the recorder.

## SECTION 3: LISTENING RESOURCES

### For Listening Practice (Free)

**TED Talks — ted.com**
- Link: https://www.ted.com/talks
- Why: Native speaker variety, academic vocabulary, accessible topics
- How to use: Listen once without subtitles. Then again with subtitles. Take notes on main points.

**BBC Podcasts — bbc.co.uk/sounds**
- Link: https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/podcasts
- Top podcasts for IELTS:
  - In Our Time (academic discussions — perfect for Section 4 prep)
  - BBC Global News Podcast (current affairs, various accents)
  - Desert Island Discs (personal stories — great for Section 2 prep)

**VOA Learning English — learningenglish.voanews.com**
- Link: https://learningenglish.voanews.com
- Why: Clear American English, transcripts available, slower speed option

**6 Minute English (BBC) — bbc.co.uk/learningenglish**
- Link: https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/6-minute-english
- Why: Short, topic-based listening with transcripts. Perfect for daily 6-minute drills.

**IELTS Listening Practice Tests — ielts-up.com**
- Link: https://ielts-up.com/listening/ielts-listening-practice.html
- Why: Multiple free tests with audio and answer keys

**New Scientist — newscientist.com**

- Why: Closest in style to real IELTS Academic passages

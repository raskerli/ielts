## PART 1: TASK 2 — ESSAY WRITING

---

## THE BAND 8 ESSAY FORMULA

Every high-scoring IELTS essay shares the same structure. Learn this. Use it every time.

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction (40–50 words)
  ↓ Paraphrase the question topic
  ↓ State your clear position or introduce the main themes
  ↓ Outline what you will discuss (optional but helps flow)

PARAGRAPH 2 — Body 1 (90–110 words)
  ↓ Main idea (one clear argument/point)
  ↓ Explain it
  ↓ Give a specific example or evidence

PARAGRAPH 3 — Body 2 (90–110 words)
  ↓ Second main idea (another argument, or counter-argument)
  ↓ Explain it
  ↓ Give a specific example or evidence

PARAGRAPH 4 — Conclusion (30–40 words)
  ↓ Summarize your main points
  ↓ Restate your position clearly
```

---

## INTRODUCTION TEMPLATES

### For Opinion Essays ("To what extent do you agree?")

**Template A — Strong agreement:**
"It is often argued that [paraphrase topic]. I entirely agree with this view, primarily because [reason 1] and [reason 2]. This essay will develop both of these arguments."

**Template B — Partial agreement:**
"The question of whether [paraphrase topic] is one that provokes considerable debate. While I accept that [opposing view has some merit], I ultimately believe that [your position] for the reasons outlined below."

### For Discussion Essays ("Discuss both views")

**Template:**
"There is ongoing debate regarding [paraphrase topic]. Proponents of [View A] argue that [short summary], whereas those who support [View B] contend that [short summary]. This essay will examine both perspectives before arriving at a reasoned conclusion."

### For Problem/Solution Essays

**Template:**
"[Paraphrase the problem] has emerged as a pressing concern in recent decades. This essay will first examine the principal causes of this issue, followed by an evaluation of the most effective potential solutions."

## BODY PARAGRAPH TEMPLATES

### The PEEL Structure

**P** = Point (state your main idea in one clear sentence)
**E** = Explain (elaborate on your point)
**E** = Evidence/Example (give a specific example, statistic, or case study)
**L** = Link (connect back to the main argument)

**Example:**

*Point:* "One of the most significant advantages of remote working is the improvement it brings to employees' work-life balance."

*Explain:* "When workers are not required to commute to an office, they reclaim hours that can be redirected towards personal activities, family commitments, and health maintenance."

*Evidence:* "A 2021 Stanford University study found that remote workers reported a 13% increase in productivity alongside significantly lower rates of burnout compared to their office-based counterparts."

*Link:* "This suggests that flexible working arrangements benefit not only individual employees but also the broader productivity of organisations."

## MODEL TASK 2 ESSAY — Band 8 Example

**Question:** *"Many people believe that the best way to improve public health is to increase the number of sports facilities. Others, however, believe that this would have little effect on public health and that other measures are required. Discuss both views and give your own opinion."*

---

**Introduction:**
The question of how to most effectively improve public health has long been a subject of debate. While some argue that expanding sports facilities is the key intervention, others contend that this alone is insufficient and that broader public health strategies are necessary. I believe both perspectives contain validity, though I find the latter view more persuasive.

**Body Paragraph 1:**
There is a reasonable case for investing in sports infrastructure. Accessible and well-maintained facilities remove a practical barrier that prevents many people from exercising regularly. In countries such as Norway and Denmark, where government investment in public gyms and recreational spaces is consistently high, obesity rates remain among the lowest globally. Furthermore, community sports centres can foster social cohesion, providing a space for individuals of all ages and backgrounds to interact, which itself contributes to improved mental wellbeing.

**Body Paragraph 2:**
Nevertheless, the expansion of physical facilities alone is unlikely to produce widespread improvements in public health. The root causes of poor health—including poor diet, sedentary work habits, and socioeconomic disadvantage—cannot be addressed by building more gyms. Evidence from several public health initiatives suggests that education campaigns, subsidised healthy food programmes, and mental health services yield more comprehensive and long-lasting improvements. For instance, Finland's ambitious "North Karelia Project" in the 1970s—which combined dietary education, smoking cessation programmes, and community health campaigns—achieved a dramatic reduction in cardiovascular disease without significant new sports infrastructure.

**Conclusion:**
In conclusion, while greater access to sports facilities can complement public health efforts, it represents only one component of a far more complex challenge. A multifaceted approach that addresses lifestyle, nutrition, and mental health simultaneously is likely to produce more meaningful results.

## PART 2: TASK 1 — ACADEMIC GRAPHS & CHARTS

---

## THE 4-PART TASK 1 STRUCTURE

```
PARAGRAPH 1 — Introduction (1–2 sentences)
  Paraphrase what the graph shows (do NOT copy)

PARAGRAPH 2 — Overview (2–3 sentences)  ← MOST IMPORTANT
  Describe the 2–3 most obvious trends WITHOUT specific data
  Examiners look for this paragraph first

PARAGRAPH 3 — Detail Group 1 (50–70 words)
  Specific data for 2–3 items + comparisons

PARAGRAPH 4 — Detail Group 2 (50–70 words)
  Specific data for remaining items + comparisons
```

---

## OVERVIEW SENTENCE TEMPLATES

For graphs (line/bar): "Overall, it is clear that [main trend], while [second main feature]. [Category X] significantly outperformed/underperformed [Category Y] throughout the period."

For pie/table: "Overall, the most notable aspect is that [largest/smallest category], with [another key contrast] also evident."

For process: "Overall, the process involves [X] main stages, beginning with [first stage] and culminating in [final output/product]."

For maps: "Overall, the most significant changes are the [main transformation], with [specific area] undergoing particularly dramatic development over the period."

---

## KEY LANGUAGE FOR GRAPHS

### Describing movement:
- Rise sharply / increase dramatically / surge
- Grow steadily / climb gradually
- Remain stable / stay relatively constant / level off
- Decline significantly / drop sharply / plummet
- Fluctuate (go up and down irregularly)
- Peak at / reach a high of / hit a maximum of
- Bottom out at / reach a low of

### Making comparisons:
- Considerably higher/lower than
- Approximately twice as high as
- Marginally more/less than
- Roughly equivalent to
- In contrast to / while / whereas

### Describing proportions (pie charts):
- accounted for / made up / constituted / represented
- The largest proportion / the smallest share / the majority

## MODEL TASK 1 RESPONSE — Band 8 Example

**Question:** *The bar chart below shows the percentage of people in different age groups who used the internet daily in one country in 2003 and 2013.*

---

**Introduction:**
The bar chart illustrates the proportion of individuals across four age groups who accessed the internet on a daily basis in 2003 and 2013 in a single country.

**Overview:**
Overall, daily internet usage increased substantially across all age groups during the decade, with younger cohorts consistently recording the highest rates of use. The most dramatic growth, however, was observed among older age groups, which saw usage more than triple over the period.

**Details 1:**
Among those aged 16–24, internet usage was already the highest in 2003 at 56%, rising to 83% by 2013. A similar, though slightly lower, pattern was evident in the 25–34 age group, where usage climbed from 41% to 74% over the same period. Both groups remained the most active internet users throughout.

**Details 2:**
In contrast, older adults began from a considerably lower base. Usage among the 35–54 group increased from 22% to 61%, while the 55+ cohort saw the most notable relative growth, rising from just 11% in 2003 to 41% in 2013 — an increase of 30 percentage points. Despite this significant rise, older adults still lagged behind younger groups in 2013.

## PROOFREADING CHECKLIST (Use Before Submitting Every Essay)

Before you put your pencil down, check:

- [ ] Did I write at least 150 words (Task 1) or 250 words (Task 2)?
- [ ] Did I answer ALL parts of the question?
- [ ] Does my introduction paraphrase the question? (Not copy it)
- [ ] Does my Task 2 have a clear overview/conclusion?
- [ ] Does my Task 1 have an overview paragraph?
- [ ] Does every body paragraph have ONE main idea?
- [ ] Did I use linking words naturally (not forced)?
- [ ] Did I use a variety of vocabulary? (No word used 3+ times)
- [ ] Did I use a variety of sentence structures?
- [ ] Can I spot any obvious grammar errors?
